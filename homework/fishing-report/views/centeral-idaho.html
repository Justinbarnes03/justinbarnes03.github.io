Centeral Idaho Report

Middle Fork Salmon River

Flies
  • Glo Bugs
  • Midges
  • Woolly Buggers
  • Egg Sucking Leech
Report

Nymphs and Woolly Buggers work well here in the winter. It's not a hatch-oriented river, but there are occasional winter midge hatches that catch the attention of trout and anglers should be prepared with the appropriate imitations.
Steelhead should be available, but they will be increasingly tough to catch as the water turns frigid. Sink-tip lines with an Egg Sucking Leech, or indicator tactics might help.

Selway River

Flies
  • Blue Wing Olives
  • Bruised Leech
  • Woolly Buggers
  • Pheasant Tails
Report

The Selway is located deep in the heart of the Frank Church Wilderness. This is not a good winter option. You will die and no one will find you. Find another river or stay home and tie some flies. If you have a death wish be sure to bring warm clothes and some BWO', Bruised Leeches, Woolly Buggars, and Pheasant Tails.

Big Wood River

Flies
  • Blue Wing Olives
  • Brassie's
  • Copper John's
  • Woolly Buggers
Report

The Big Wood below Ketchum can have decent blue-winged olive hatches in December. The blue-wings are typically size 20-22 and are matched with a Sparkle Dun. As the weather gets cold, midges will dominate the action.
Size 16-18 red Copper Johns or red Brassies are a good fly choice, too, and it doesn't hurt to carry some size 8-10 olive Woolly Buggers to use in the deeper water. Make sure your nymphs are on the bottom.

Silver Creek

Flies
  • Blue Wing Olive
  • Prince Nymph
  • Articulated Dungons
  • Zebra Midge
Report

The Conservancy water is closed. Downstream from there, look for hatches of blue-winged olives and midges. Look for rising fish; these are not spawners. Streamers, midges, and blue-winged olives are good choices here.
